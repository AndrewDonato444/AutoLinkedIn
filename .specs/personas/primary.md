# Outbound Founder

## Context

- Runs a small B2B company (1-20 people), wears the sales hat themselves or has a tiny sales team
- Day is split between product, calls, and pipeline — outreach is important but competes with everything else
- Laptop-primary, checks GojiBerry dashboard and Claude conversations from browser
- Technically comfortable — can set up an MCP connector, edit a .env file, write a prompt — but doesn't want to write code
- Juggles LinkedIn, CRM, email, Slack, calendar; outreach is one of 10 things fighting for attention

## What They Care About

- **Meetings booked** — the only metric that matters. Everything upstream (leads found, messages sent, reply rate) is a means to this end
- Spending less than 15 minutes/day on outreach while maintaining or increasing pipeline
- Messages that sound like they actually read the person's profile, not "I noticed we're in the same industry"
- Knowing which leads are worth their time before they invest attention

## What Frustrates Them

- Reviewing 50 leads and realizing most are garbage — wasted 30 minutes
- Messages that sound robotic or templated (they've been on the receiving end)
- Having to context-switch into "outreach mode" — open LinkedIn, open a spreadsheet, cross-reference, draft, send
- Systems that require babysitting — if it needs daily manual steps, they'll stop using it within a week
- Getting rate-limited or flagged on LinkedIn because automation was too aggressive

## Their Vocabulary

- "leads" not "contacts" or "prospects" (until they reply, then they're "prospects")
- "campaign" not "sequence" or "cadence"
- "reply rate" not "engagement rate"
- "ICP" or "ideal customer" not "target segment"
- "intent" or "buying signals" not "lead score"
- "warm" not "marketing qualified"
- "pipeline" not "opportunity funnel"
- "personalized" not "enriched"

## Patience Level

Very Low — They'll give the system one honest shot (30 min setup, review first batch of leads). If the first campaign doesn't clearly outperform what they were doing manually, they'll go back to their old workflow. They have zero patience for configuration sprawl or debugging automations.

## Success Metric

"I wake up, spend 5 minutes reviewing leads and messages, approve the good ones, and get replies by end of week — without touching LinkedIn directly."
